\u201cWe believe profitability and sustainability can go hand-in-hand. Sunway Group, with almost 50 years of track record, and aspirations to surpass a century and beyond, believes that corporate longevity and a commitment to the Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) agenda are intertwined and form the bedrock of the Group\u2019s sustainability agenda.<\/p>\n

This approach sets Sunway apart from its competitors.<\/p>\n

Over the years, Sunway has striven to stay agile, innovative and relevant in the ever-evolving business landscape, which has helped propel the Group into a 16,000-strong staff with 13 business divisions across more than 50 locations worldwide.<\/p>\n

                          Transformation Spanning Five Decades and Counting<\/h3>\n

                          Growing up in the former tin-mining town of Pusing, Perak, the ugly scars left behind by disused mining pools left a deep impression on Tan Sri Sir Jeffrey Cheah.<\/p><\/div>\n

                          Growing up in the small former tin-mining town of Pusing in Perak, Tan Sri Sir Jeffrey Cheah witnessed first-hand the impact of environmental degradation and the ugly scars of destructive mining activities all around him.<\/p>\n

                          This experience shaped Tan Sri Sir Jeffrey Cheah’s vision to build a vibrant, integrated and sustainable township.<\/p>\n

                          From its humble beginnings<\/a> in quarrying and tin-mining, Sunway has transformed into a regional conglomerate developing integrated townships, making quality education accessible, providing world-class healthcare services and championing responsible business practices.<\/p>\n

                          Sunway Group founder and chairman Tan Sri Sir Jeffrey Cheah articulating his vision of an integrated township at the 800-acre piece of abandoned wasteland where Sunway City Kuala Lumpur sits today.<\/p><\/div>\n

                          The construction of Sunway City Kuala Lumpur saw the rehabilitation of an 800-acre wasteland of abandoned mining pools into a thriving wonderland. The city today comprises residential properties, commercial offices, educational institutions, healthcare facilities as well as theme parks and retail malls.<\/p>\n

                          These physical structures are all located within the burgeoning green tapestry<\/a> of a 15-minute city, which creates a symbiotic relationship among the various amenities within the township. This model of urban mobility and connectivity<\/a> now serves as a blueprint for other Sunway townships throughout the country.<\/p>\n

                          Sunway City Kuala Lumpur is today home to a vibrant community of more than 200,000 living, working, playing and studying in a safe, connected and healthy environment, epitomising Sunway\u2019s vision to be Asia\u2019s model corporation, innovating to enrich lives for a better tomorrow.<\/p>\n

                          The Sustainable Intelligent Transport Ecosystem (SITE) within Sunway City Kuala Lumpur, comprising the 4.3-km elevated covered walkway and the electric-powered Bus Rapid Transit on specially designed elevated lanes, among others, underpins the seamless urban connectivity within this 15-minute city.<\/p><\/div>\n

                          Having pledged to achieve net zero carbon emissions by 2050, Sunway has since taken its commitment to the sustainability agenda one step further by introducing an internal carbon pricing framework towards this end. It is the first in Malaysia, and one of the first in Asia, to implement such a carbon reduction strategy across its businesses.<\/p>\n

                          In addition, the Group also established the nation\u2019s first Green Lease Partnership programme of its kind via its real estate investment trust division, Sunway REIT.<\/p>\n

                          A Transformation Office has been established to ensure Sunway\u2019s core businesses remain relevant in a rapidly evolving business landscape.<\/p><\/div>\n

                          In the lead up to Sunway\u2019s 50th anniversary in 2024, a Transformation Office has been established to anticipate and address the myriad challenges in the decades ahead.<\/p>\n

                          Led by Evan Cheah, the group\u2019s CEO of digital and strategic investments, the Transformation Office comprises a team of young leaders across various disciplines who will drive innovation across the Group to inculcate fresh ideas, unique perspectives and future-oriented thinking to guide Sunway\u2019s transformation agenda.<\/p>\n

                          The ESG Approach to Sustainable Business<\/h3>\n

                          Although earliest discussions on ESG can be traced to the early 2000s, the ESG agenda only came to the fore and gained mainstream prominence in 2015 due to two major global events \u2013 the ratification of the Paris Agreement, an international treaty on climate change, as well as the 17 Sustainable Development Goals, a 15-year shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, adopted by the United Nations.<\/p>\n

                          For investors and other stakeholders, the ESG framework offers a data-based mechanism to evaluate a company\u2019s performance on sustainability initiatives, impact on society and treatment of employees, as well as its management of ethical issues.<\/p>\n

                          It is also a framework that allows companies to assess its business risks and opportunities, as well as shift an over emphasis on bottom-line considerations to non-financial indicators for a more holistic assessment of qualitative progress.<\/p>\n

                          \u201cAt Sunway, we view ESG as a disclosure framework that fosters transparency. Disclosures make companies transparent, and transparency brings about accountability. In other words, transparency builds trust. This is why Sunway is firmly committed to the ESG agenda, Mr. Ong says.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

                          Through disclosures, stakeholders are able to see how companies are responding to pressing environmental, social and governance issues, which concerns all stakeholders.<\/p>\n

                          Companies that prioritise their environmental and social impact are more likely to thrive in the long run. Sunway’s sustainability agenda, with its two-pronged approach of survival and responsibility, reflects the Group’s commitment to making a positive impact in society.<\/p>\n
