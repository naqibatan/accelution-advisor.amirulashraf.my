Stakeholder Engagement

Our Stakeholders

We define our stakeholders as groups whom our business has a significant impact on and those with a vested interest in our operations. We believe that actively engaging and working in partnership with our stakeholders is crucial to addressing sustainability concerns.

Customers

We value our customers and strive to serve with the best of our abilities and resources to provide them quality products and services.

Expectation
  • Product quality and safety
  • Innovation
  • Customer welfare
  • Ecological impact
  • Training and education
  • Waste and hazardous waste management
  • Water and effluents management
Our approach
  • We place innovation at the forefront as one of our strategic growth pillars. The various divisions strive towards innovation, product quality and safety as well as customer service excellence in order to maintain their competitive edge as well as to accelerate customer engagement during the pandemic.
  • Our sustainability report addresses our environmental impact in a comprehensive and transparent manner.
  • We have installed a cybersecurity system to mitigate cyberattacks and to protect the data privacy of our stakeholders.
  • We ensure our employees are well equipped with the latest knowledge and skills to meet customer expectations.

Employees

We prioritise the welfare of our employees, believing that they act as enablers of the transformation we want to achieve within the organisation, in the communities we build, own, manage and serve.

Expectation
  • Employee engagement
  • Employee health and safety
  • Training and education
  • Labour practices
Our approach
  • To future-proof our workforce, we provide holistic and structured training and development programmes that help employees achieve their personal goals as well as the Group’s collective goals as an organisation.
  • As members of the community they serve, live, learn and play in, our employees enjoy a wide range of benefits, ranging from childcare subsidies to staff discounts for a whole host of services.

Government & Industry Affiliates

We work closely with the government, such as the Department of Occupational Safety and Health (DOSH), and industry affiliates to achieve the best outcomes in sustainable development and nation-building efforts.

Expectation
  • Business ethics
  • Ecological impact
  • Economic performance
  • Employee health and safety
  • Labour practices
Our approach
  • We uphold good corporate governance through a range of robust policies, namely, the Code of Conduct and Business Ethics as well as the Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy to ensure regulatory compliance.
  • We support the government’s Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) as we seek to progress towards low-carbon sustainable cities through sustainable development and acceleration of digital transformation.
  • We support the Ministry of Housing and Local Government’s recommendations on Smart and Sustainable Cities.
  • We support, collaborate and play active roles in industry affiliations to steer policy changes and industry initiatives to contribute to nation-building and economic prosperity.

Local Communities & NGOS

We invest in the communities in which we operate and aim to create long-term value to enrich their livelihoods.

Expectation
  • Business model resilience
  • Community
  • Physical impact of climate change
  • Waste and hazardous materials management
  • Water and effluents management
Our approach
  • We recorded more than 18,000 man-hours of employee volunteerism in #SunwayforGood and CSR-related programmes.
  • We took proactive measures to respond to COVID-19 impact, contributing to frontline workers, lifting the underprivileged as well as our mall tenants whose businesses were impacted by the pandemic.
  • We worked together with the communities to advocate waste reduction while improving their livelihoods.

Shareholders

We maintain prudent risk management and maximise our shareholders’ returns on top of ensuring transparency through fair reporting and timely communication.

Expectation
  • Business model resilience
  • Business ethics
  • Ecological impact
  • Economic performance
  • Systematic risk management
Our approach
  • We embedded the recommendations of the Task Force on Climate related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) to bolster the Group’s risk management in climate change.
  • We implement strategies to enhance business and financial resilience.
  • We conduct active investor relations programmes and activities to engage with the investment community and retail investors.

Suppliers/Vendors

We ensure our suppliers adhere to our Sustainable Procurement Policy and Supplier Code of Conduct, which emphasise environmental protection and social ethics.

Expectation
  • Economic performance
  • Employee health and safety
  • Human rights
  • Labour practices
  • Training and education
Our approach
  • We developed a Sustainable Procurement Policy to prioritise suppliers and vendors who adopt sustainable practices in their organisations.
  • We practise equal access to purchasing opportunities, ensuring a transparent tendering and bidding process in the procurement value chain.
  • Our procurement registration form and terms & conditions are easily accessible via our corporate website at amirulashraf.my.
  • We utilise Esker software to ease the approval process and eliminate hard copy submissions from our suppliers to maintain a systematic archive and to improve customer experience.
  • Sunway respects human rights in alignment with the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights and UN Global Compact (UNGC) Principles. We also recognise the International Labour Organization’s (ILO) eight fundamental Conventions concerning freedom of association and the right to collective bargaining, the elimination of forced labour, child labour and discrimination in the workplace.
